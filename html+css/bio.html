Welcome. I am Minjung. I'm a doctoral student at Temple's Fox MIS program.

Before that, I was a good student (believe it or not) in a Master's program at Seoul National University (2009-2011). During that time, I was an editor for university student press and voluntary English teacher for the underrepresented kids outside the campus. Of course, I did more "exciting" things such as writing six papers and one book chapter, and taking doctoral-level classes and research seminars.

Before that, I was a not-so-good undergrad student at the same university, where I had not realized I'd pursue another degree later on. During that time, I played guitar for an amateur band, performed in a drama club, and traveled around the world as a backpacker. Of course, I did more "exciting" projects such as working as intern, and building an online community for the university.

Before that, I was an exchange student in HEC Paris (2007-2008), France's most prestigious grande ecole specializing in business studies. During that time, I enjoyed a wide variety of wine and cheese, making the best use of cultural accomplishment of the country. Of course, I tried to let others enjoy my own accomplishment, too, presenting at a city-sponsored business competition as a final runner-up.

Before that, I dreamed of studying literature in college or being a fiction writer. During that time, I occasionally entertained my friends as an aspring story teller. Of course, my stories continuted until we finished the national college entrance exam. Given the percentile of 99.9%, I realized going to college would be a better bet for me than becoming a good writer.

Before that, I attended, no, actually lived at a private boarding school for almost three years (2000-2003). During that time, I was an avid user of an online social network that used to be Korea's no. 1, Alexa's no. 3 website. It literally connected me and the outside world. Of course, I'm thankful that I could directly observe the burst of networking phenomena in a country where the Internet usage was record high.

Before that, I started building my homepage with simple HTML and exchanging emails with friends from all over the world at junior high (1997-2000). During that time, I created my first email account at Yahoo! while my favorite search engine was AltaVista. Of course, I vividly remember those endless nights that greeted me with modem beeps and Telnet's black screen.

Before that, I recall I began learning English as a kid from my mother who was teaching at high school (late 1980's-early 1990's). During that time, I somehow thought upper case alphabets are prettier than the lower case counterparts -- for that reason, I once wrote down Michael Jackson's lyrics in all capital letters. Of course, I didn't do it when I began coding in GW-BASIC, my first programming language. (If you remember numbering command lines and "Run" escaper, we have a lot more to say.)

Before that, I was born as a much celebrated honeymoon baby. During that time, my father's side of grandfather made a name for this new born while my mother's side relatives sent a person to get advice from a fortune teller. Coincidentally, and still to my surprise, both sides came up with the same name, Minjung. Of course, I appreciate what my name's supposed to mean: eternal strength.
