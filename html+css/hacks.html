1. CyclePhilly

Computer Science people commonly call themselves “hackers” and software “hacks,” meaning by no means illegal in this sense. Also in civic innovation movement where programmers voluntarily gather to solve urban problems through technology and open data, we call ourselves civic hackers. In Code for Philly (Philadelphia brigade of Code for America), I mainly helped this project called CyclePhilly. This is an interactive map, both web and mobile app, where users can track and share bike routes using GPS info.

The map is aimed to help record trips real time, find the fastest and safest way, and eventually prevent accidents related to bikers on the road. The project was helped by many other young professionals in the city sometimes connected through “hackathons,” which is an open source coding competition. If you’re in Philly, check it out and leave feedback.

Android app: https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.phillyopen.mytracks.cyclephilly
iOS app: https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/cyclephilly/id850035510
Website: http://www.cyclephilly.org/

2. MIS Job Placement

Although adjacent disciplines like Economics and Marketing have websites or student-initiated projects dedicated to their job market, we don’t have one in MIS. I have collected information about MIS PhDs from the year I began Master’s degree in 2009. It is currently more than 1,200 individuals’ PhD granting institution, advisor, initial placement, subsequent employment(s), and respective years.

It started purely out of curiosity and personal need but I now intend to open this data for good. The data is longitudinal so I’m hoping not only job candidates but also potential employers can benefit. In addition to front-end dev, however, I welcome collaboration in transforming this data set into a more sharable and readable form. If you’re interested, feel free to contact me.
