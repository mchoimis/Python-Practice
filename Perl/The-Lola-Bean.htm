    3/28/2011 2 check-ins here

    I can't wait to move back around the corner from this place in a month!

    When the Lola Bean was first being put together, I was confused as to why we needed yet ANOTHER coffee shop in the same area, but this place was a great addition. It seems that every coffee house in Fishtown has a completely different vibe to offer and clientele to serve and I like it.

    Lola Bean is clean and modern and sleek. It's painted with bright pink and green. The furniture is a great blend of vintage and modern. I'd like to describe this place as .... sassy.

    A small coffee is $1.50 but I ended strolling back in after an appointment and they refilled the same cup for only $1.

    Can't wait for this to become my go-to coffee shop for long grueling afternoons of studying between jobs.

    • Elite '13
    • 743 friends

    • Bill M.

    Philadelphia, PA

    1/20/2011 5 check-ins here

    It's kind of funny - there was a time about a year and a half ago where I was feeling down on Rocket Cat being the only coffeehouse in Fishtown (excluding NoLibs). Fast forward to Milk Crate Cafe, Coffeehouse (I will find you hardest to find coffeehouse on Yelp!), and here opening within a fairly short time of each other; and it almost feels like someone was listening to me. But with the vibrant and eclectic coffeehouse scene that Fishtown has thus become, I consider that a victory for all and not just myself. And I view Lola Bean as one of those spots to watch in growth.

    I first heard about about the Bean on Thrillist and Meal Ticket and marveled over the prospect of grabbing some fresh Au Fournil pastries and eventually soup over a nice cup of joe (yet another La Colombe house in Philly, but in this case its a welcome sort of imperfection). and when I did so I found it to be a welcome occasion.

    It's exterior has a vast number of windows, and one of them overlooks a welcoming looking side garden. While it makes here the perfect coffeehouse destination for gorgeous spring and summer days, it also makes it kind of sombering during dismal fall and winter days. But at least it gives you the opportunity to warm up to soups like potato leek and chicken matzo ball. throw in things like carrot cake squares, and red velvet whoopie pies; and you have plenty of reasons to indulge your sweet tooth.

    The Lola Bean also has some pretty interesting art viewings, and sells  some albums from some local favorites.

    Philadelphia, PA

    1/31/2011

    I was invited for a Sunday coffee date and my friend wanted to come out to see Fishtown. He asked me where we should meet for coffee. And I wanted to go off my usual Cafeito or Rocket Cat path, so I suggested we try out The Lola Bean.

    Nothing like drinking coffee during the nervous moments of a first date. But that's how I like it- might as well amp up the excitement with a huge cup of coffee. And that's what I did, ordered the large. He was more moderate and had a small Americano. My coffee came to $2.50 and was placed on the counter and lids were right there. That's a simple and helpful advantage over the Rocket Cat, which requires you to grab a lid in advance or walk it to the milk/sugar table.

    The Lola Bean was smaller and more intimate than I expected, but we were able to secure the last table by the large windows. The sun was beginning to set and I was bathed in sunlight. We dawdled over one cup of coffee, but had the good sense to leave before it closed at 4 pm. The atmosphere is conducive to close, private conversation, although I don't doubt that everyone heard every word. The place seems to be meant for quiet reflection, rather than boisterous talk.

    Pink walls (I'm a sucker for a good pink wall), giant windows, amazing coffee, and DELICIOUS pastries!  Welcome to the neighborhood The Lola Bean!  

    With several tables and seriously gigantic windows you can sit and people watch, read a book, use the complimentary wifi and drink yummy drinks.  I've had both a regular coffee with a shot of espresso and a latte, and both were delicious.  I've also tried a chocolate croissant, a croissant reminiscent of a coffee roll or cinnamon bun and some amazing tarts.  What? I liked baked goods.  Don't judge me, sweater weather is coming.

    The overall look and feel of this place is happy, as are the staff/owners.  With the pink and green paint, the gorgeous white furniture and those light fixtures! I've got to get me some of those.  You can tell a lot of love and planning went into the decor of this place.  The atmosphere alone is a pick me up before you even get your caffeine jolt bit it's lovely to see it transcend into the product as well, quality stuff people, quality stuff.  Even if the walls weren't pink I'd still totally be crushing on this place.
