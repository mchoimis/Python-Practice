    Philadelphia, PA

    8/24/2013 10 check-ins here

    When I think of my neighborhood and how it has changed in the last eight years since I have moved here, I think of One Shot.   It really embodies Northern Liberties and has evolved much in the same way our neighborhood has.  Once tucked away on Liberties walk and a bit hidden and flying under the radar, it moved to a new location on West George and American and it just blossomed, grew into its own.  Much like our neighborhood.

    I tend to wax poetically about small businesses that I really love, and One Shot is no exception.  I also have quite a warm spot in my heart for the place too, as it was where my baby shower took place.  Yep!  Did you know that One Shot's second floor can be rented for private events?  It is absolute perfection for a small, intimate affair. (baby or bridal shower, birthday party, special luncheon just because?) From soup to nuts, One Shot and owner Rebecca made it the most splendid, adorable affair.  The whole event went without a hitch, the service was impeccable, friendly and the menu crafted especially for the event was perfection (there was this caramel pot de crème, and a small paté chinois- shepherd's pie - a nod to my french canadian heritage, and the most yummy macarons, amongst other delicious tidbits that were to die for!).  

    It doesn't hurt that the whole place is so uniquely gorgeous.  The artwork, the lighting, repurposed vintage items, the open space design that still makes the place feel cozy and personal, the beautiful bathrooms (seriously beautiful bathrooms), the cute curved booths on the first floor and the seating upstairs, it just works, you know?  

    The coffee is super strong  (like I like it) and the food is killer.  Go there early in the morning and experience their baked goods hot and fresh out of the oven.  This morning, for example, had a blueberry and peach scone that rocked my world.  It's the little things.  All their baked goods are super yummy, and don't get me started on their breakfast, brunch menu - sweet baby jesus - their ultimate breakfast sandwich contains some of my most favorite items on earth: a brioche bun, brie, bacon, hash brown and two fried eggs.  Hello lover.  Breakfast of champions right there.  Just give it a whirl, you won't ever look back.

    It's the perfect place to grab a quick dose of caffeine, or while away your hours as you nibble on a croissant and get caught up on your blog lovin', or go upstairs where it's quieter with one of their daily crafted non-caffeinated options and catch up on your reading (with a book, not a kindle).

    Beautiful coffeeshop in the heart of NoLibs. It looks pretty much exactly like my mid-90s fantasy of what a really hip and sophisticated neighborhood coffee shop would be like. (Yes, that's a good thing.)

    Eclectic, shabby-chic, vintage decor? Check. Pierced, tatted, stylin' staff? Check. Cool, brainy-seeming clientele? Check. Cozy upstairs space with room for acoustic performers? Check. Couches? Check. Massive lending library with hundreds of books? Check! I could go on, but you get the idea.

    I started off wanting to give this place four stars but decided to up it to five because this place is so much the archetype of what I want in a chill coffeeshop to hang out in but rarely find.

    The service was slightly haphazard, but it all worked out in the end and the staff were pretty cheerful and friendly. The food was good and I enjoyed my cappuccino. I came here to see my friend's band (High Hearts, they're amazing) and thought the set-up was really nice for that. The act sets up in an area upstairs with comfy couches for audience members to sit, and then there are also tons of tables throughout the room. It was an acoustic set and the sound was really nice up there.

    Randomly, I am pretty much obsessed with the bookshelves upstairs. They're a neat vintage/industrial/DIY set-up of wooden boards and metal piping that's then bolted to the wall. They look amazing.

About This Business

Provided by business

Specialties

One Shot Cafe proudly serves Stumptown Coffee in our carefully crafted coffee drinks and features locally roasted coffee purveyors such as ReAnimator and Rival Bros Coffee by the bag. The cafe menu features New American brunch dishes that a showcase some decidedly international twists, as well as everyone's classic brunch favorites. Weekly specials, grab-and-go meals and snacks, and an outstanding selection of house-made pastry and desserts are offered at the counter. Customers can enjoy fare on two levels of the extensively renovated space, handsomely adorned with refreshed family heirlooms, Philadelphia industrial antiques, and furniture by local artisans.

History

Established in 2005

One Shot started as a small coffee shop in the newly constructed Liberties Walk of North 2nd street in 2005, however we quickly grew out of our old 20+ seat space.  In 2011, we purchased a 3 story former bar, two doors from our original location.  With the help of SLDesign and Buckminster Green builders, we remodeled the entire structure to fit our style and sentiment.  Using reclaimed lumber as rustic accents the extensively renovated space is handsomely adorned with refreshed family heirlooms, Philadelphia industrial antiques, and custom built furniture by local artisans. We proudly serve Stumptown Coffee,  as well as a  full brunch menu of seasonal fare in our 82+ seats and two floors of space . Our new home has proved to be an amazing neighborhood addition, and a cafe that truly everyone can enjoy.

Meet the Business Owner: Melissa and Rebecca B.

Barista, Foodie, and Northern Liberties inhabitant.
