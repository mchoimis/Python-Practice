    • Foodimus M.

    Philadelphia, PA

    9/15/2013 8 check-ins here

    This place is all about the coffee, and that's what I expect at a coffee shop. The staff are very friendly and patient, usually more so than the customers who are jostling for position in line and walking into each other smacking their backpack into your face. It's a tiny location, especially on the ordering/counter side, so enter with caution. Sometimes the bathroom and the prep counter for sugar/cream/straws gets a little messy but I've only noticed that at times when the customer population is swollen inside, all the seats are taken, and there's a line...so it's completely understandable that's not a priority. I like that you can just step outside to the tables on Liacouras Walk if you don't want to stand around with the undergrads "doing homework" at tables inside. Think they have free wifi too, but then, the entire Temple campus has wifi...so...I couldn't really tell you.

    They also seem to go out of their way lately to add on sugar-free flavors and advertise a low-fat and/or sugar-free variety of whatever the seasonal special drinks are. Best place on campus for a caffeine fix.

  • Review from Lauren D.

    • 103 friends
    • 108 reviews
    • Lauren D.

    Philadelphia, PA

    11/27/2010

    I never ever thought I would say this in my life, but I definitely would take Starbucks over this place. I just don't really dig the vibe of this place as a whole. I suppose it could be a decent place to study and yes, they do play infinitely better music than some places but it's just too bright and too obviously a chain. Meh.

    Their coffee is completely all over the board and usually makes my stomach feel all sour after I drink it for some reason. Although I do typically order a red eye so it's probably the espresso-coffee combination. In terms of lattes, they have a whole bunch of creative and sweet concoctions to choose from and many of them can be made with sugar-free syrups for those of us that are calorie conscious (ahem). Unfortunately, those sugar-free syrups are absolutely disgusting and are wayyyyy too sweet. I've also had a latte or two get screwed up - most notably when I ordered 3 shots of espresso in a small and noticed that while I had paid for all 3, the cup was marked with only 1 shot. Unfortunately I was already on the subway when I noticed the error so going back was kind of not an option. Bummer :[

    The staff is super hip and apathetic. They're also all super cute too so at least you'll have some eye candy to look at while you watch them begrudgingly make your beverage.

    Prices? On par with starbucks. I'm not sure about there FroYo prices because ew, who eats that for breakfast, I'm sure they're on par with other random places.

    In short? Stick with regular coffee (oh and if you prefer to sweeten it with Splenda, there is a basket of it where you pick up your beverage. Why they can't move it back with all the other sugars, sweeteners, and creamers is beyond me), don't get the sugar-free stuff, and bask in the hipster glory of this ironic chain coffee shop.

    Oh and as a fun side note: if you're in the restaurant industry and troll Craigslist for jobs, be sure to read their job postings for a good laugh. I particularly enjoyed knowing that they were more of an "...Arcade Fire coffee house..."

    Listed in: The outcasts

    Philadelphia, PA

    3/5/2013

    As the other reviews have stated, this place is INFINITELY better than Starbucks. As a barista, I sometimes wish that I can jump behind the counter and make my own drink, but seeing as this is not an option (for good reason), I do my best to describe my drink of choice to the baristas. They never shoot me a snide look and instead ask me about my steamed milk to coffee to syrup ratio in my flavored au lait. This is one of the reasons why I tip well. I sometimes go for the lattes, which have been good for the most part, but a couple of times the espresso tasted burnt or it was delivered to me cold. As a barista, I understand that shit happens, but simultaneously as a good and familiar customer I would like to see a little bit more consistency. I don't usually hang around to sit down and hang out (always in that working student rush) but this is definitely a more appealing place to do so than Dunkin or one of the TWO bad Starbucks in a 3-block radius of campus.
    And yes yes, attractive staff ftw. But they're also nice, so tip them the next time you go.
